%% ── Abstract ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{abstract}
We introduce and validate a \emph{scope gate}: an automatic decision procedure
that classifies a quantum many-body system as either amenable to
holographic tensor-network methods (\inscope) or not (\outscope) based
solely on the finite-size scaling of half-chain entanglement entropy $S(L)$.
The criterion rests on Akaike's Information Criterion with correction (AICc)
model selection between a logarithmic (Calabrese–Cardy) model and an
exponentially-saturating model.  When $\dAICc \equiv \mathrm{AICc}_{\mathrm{sat}}
- \mathrm{AICc}_{\log} \le -2$ the saturating model is favoured and the system
is classified \inscope; when $\dAICc \ge +2$ the log model is favoured and the
system is classified \outscope.  We validate the gate on the $S=\half$ XXZ
spin chain, computing $S(L)$ by exact diagonalization (ED) at $L=8,12,16$
and density-matrix renormalization group (DMRG) at $L=20,\ldots,44$
($L \bmod 4 = 0$ throughout) for anisotropies $\Delta = 0.5$ and $\Delta = 2.0$.
The ED and DMRG data agree to relative precision $< 10^{-8}$ at shared system
sizes, confirming their poolability.  For $\Delta = 0.5$ (critical XY phase)
the log model is overwhelmingly preferred ($\dAICc = +20.1$, \outscope),
while for $\Delta = 2.0$ (gapped \Neel{} phase) the saturating model is
preferred ($\dAICc = -21.1$, \inscope).  Near the Kosterlitz–Thouless
transition ($\Delta \approx 1$) the gate correctly returns \inconclusive
because the exponentially large correlation length $\xi_{\rm KT} \sim
e^{\pi/\sqrt{\Delta-1}}$ exceeds the accessible system sizes, an outcome that
is physically correct rather than a failure.  The AICc's finite-sample
correction provides a natural safeguard: when data are too sparse to constrain
the saturating model ($n \le 4$ for a three-parameter fit), the penalty term
diverges and the gate is forced to return \inconclusive rather than a false
positive.  These results establish entanglement entropy scaling as a
reliable, numerically inexpensive pre-screening tool for selecting appropriate
computational methods.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Tensor-network methods such as matrix product states (MPS) and the
multi-scale entanglement renormalization ansatz (MERA) achieve their
efficiency through the assumption that entanglement in the target state is
geometrically constrained—specifically, that the half-chain entanglement
entropy $S$ saturates to a finite constant as the system size grows
\cite{Vidal2003,Vidal2007,Orus2014}.  For gapped one-dimensional systems the
area law $S \sim \text{const}$ holds by the Hastings theorem
\cite{Hastings2007}, whereas critical systems with a conformal field theory
(CFT) description obey the Calabrese–Cardy (CC) logarithm
$S(L) = \frac{c}{3}\ln L + \text{const}$ for periodic boundaries, or
$\frac{c}{6}\ln L$ for open boundaries \cite{Calabrese2004}.

A natural question is whether one can \emph{automatically} decide, before
investing in a full tensor-network simulation, whether a system will be
well-approximated by area-law states.  Such a decision procedure—a
\emph{scope gate}—would be valuable both for automated simulation pipelines
and for exploratory studies of phase diagrams.

In this work we design a scope gate based on finite-size scaling of the
half-chain entropy $S(L)$.  The gate fits two competing models to $S(L)$
data:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \modeltag{Log}: $S(L) = \alpha \ln L + \beta$ (consistent with
        criticality, \outscope for area-law methods);
  \item \modeltag{Sat}: $S(L) = \Sinf - A\,e^{-L/\xi}$ (consistent with
        a gapped phase, \inscope for area-law methods).
\end{itemize}
Model selection is performed using AICc \cite{Akaike1974,Hurvich1989}, which
balances goodness-of-fit against model complexity and includes a
finite-sample correction that becomes singular when data are insufficient
to constrain the more complex model.

We validate the gate on the spin-$\half$ XXZ chain
\cite{Bethe1931,Orbach1958,Walker1959}, which has a well-understood phase
diagram: a gapless XY phase for $|\Delta| < 1$, a gapped \Neel{} phase for
$\Delta > 1$, and a Kosterlitz–Thouless (KT) transition at $\Delta = 1$
\cite{KT1972,KT1973}.  This chain provides a clean testbed because both
phases and the transition are analytically understood, so the gate's outputs
can be compared against known ground truth.

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:model} describes the
XXZ chain and its known phase structure.  Section~\ref{sec:methods} details
the computational methods (ED, DMRG, data pooling).  Section~\ref{sec:gate}
defines the scope gate and its AICc criterion.  Section~\ref{sec:results}
presents the numerical validation.  Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses
the gate's properties, limitations, and connections to holography.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

\section{The Spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ XXZ Chain}
\label{sec:model}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The XXZ Hamiltonian on $L$ sites with open boundary conditions (OBC) is
\begin{equation}
  H = J\sum_{i=1}^{L-1}
      \left(S^x_i S^x_{i+1} + S^y_i S^y_{i+1} + \Delta\, S^z_i S^z_{i+1}\right),
  \label{eq:xxz}
\end{equation}
where $S^\alpha_i = \sigma^\alpha_i/2$, $J = 1$ (antiferromagnetic
coupling), and $\Delta \ge 0$ is the Ising anisotropy.

\subsection*{Phase diagram}

The model is exactly solvable via the Bethe ansatz \cite{Bethe1931}.
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\Delta < 1$: Gapless XY phase.  The low-energy physics is described
    by a free compact boson CFT with central charge $c = 1$
    \cite{Lukyanov1997}.  The half-chain entropy scales logarithmically,
    $S(L) \sim \frac{1}{6}\ln L + \text{const}$ (OBC).
  \item $\Delta > 1$: Gapped \Neel{} (antiferromagnetic Ising) phase.
    The gap $\Delta E > 0$ implies an area-law ground state with $S$ saturating
    to a finite $\Sinf$ as $L\to\infty$.
  \item $\Delta = 1$: Kosterlitz–Thouless (KT) transition
    \cite{KT1972,KT1973}.  The gap closes exponentially,
    $\xi_{\rm KT} \sim \exp\!\left(\pi/\sqrt{\Delta - 1}\right)$, making the
    correlation length extremely large for $\Delta$ near 1.
\end{itemize}

The well-known phase structure makes the XXZ chain an ideal validation target:
the ground truth is known exactly, so the gate's decisions can be compared
unambiguously.

\section{Computational Methods}
\label{sec:methods}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Exact Diagonalization}

For small system sizes $L \le 16$ we compute the ground-state vector
$|\psi_0\rangle$ exactly via sparse Lanczos diagonalization in the
$S^z_{\rm tot} = 0$ sector (which contains the ground state for the
antiferromagnetic XXZ chain with OBC).  The Hamiltonian is represented in
the occupation basis $\{|\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_L\rangle\}$ with
$\sigma_i \in \{\uparrow,\downarrow\}$.  Ground-state energies for
representative cases are given in Table~\ref{tab:energies}.

The half-chain entanglement entropy is computed as
\begin{equation}
  S(L) = -\Tr_A[\rho_A \ln \rho_A],
  \label{eq:vne}
\end{equation}
where $\rho_A = \Tr_B |\psi_0\rangle\langle\psi_0|$ is the reduced density
matrix of the left half ($\ell = L/2$ sites).

\subsection{Density-Matrix Renormalization Group}

For $L > 16$ we use DMRG with a maximum bond dimension $\chi = 512$ and
single-site sweeps until the energy variance $\langle H^2\rangle - \langle H\rangle^2 < 10^{-12}$.
The DMRG entropy values used here are pre-computed high-accuracy benchmarks
from the \texttt{dmrg\_overlap\_high\_accuracy\_L4k\_bulk} dataset
(see Table~\ref{tab:dmrg_data}).

\subsection{ED--DMRG Overlap Calibration and Data Pooling}

Before pooling ED and DMRG data we verify that the two methods agree at
shared system sizes $L = 8, 12, 16$.  The overlap criterion requires
\begin{equation}
  |S_{\rm ED}(L) - S_{\rm DMRG}(L)| < 10^{-3}
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \frac{|S_{\rm ED}(L) - S_{\rm DMRG}(L)|}{S_{\rm DMRG}(L)} < 10^{-3}
  \label{eq:overlap}
\end{equation}
to hold simultaneously at all three calibration sizes.  Table~\ref{tab:cal}
reports the calibration results; all errors are $< 10^{-8}$.

\subsection{Parity Protocol: $L \bmod 4 = 0$}

Half-chain entanglement entropy in spin-$\half$ chains with OBC exhibits
an even/odd parity oscillation: $S(L)$ at sizes $L \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$
differs systematically from $S(L)$ at $L \equiv 0 \pmod{4}$ because the
Hamiltonian ground state changes its total spin parity.  Mixing both
families in a single fit introduces a spurious alternating signal that can
be absorbed by an oscillation-corrected log model, causing the gapped phase
to be misclassified as critical (Section~\ref{sec:parity}).  We therefore
restrict all $L$ values to satisfy $L \bmod 4 = 0$:
$L \in \{8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 36, 40, 44\}$ ($n = 10$ points per
$\Delta$).

\section{The AICc Scope Gate}
\label{sec:gate}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Competing Models}

We fit two parametric models to the pooled $S(L)$ data:
\begin{align}
  \text{\modeltag{Log}}: & \quad S(L) = \alpha \ln L + \beta,
    \label{eq:log} \\
  \text{\modeltag{Sat}}: & \quad S(L) = \Sinf - A\,e^{-L/\xi},
    \label{eq:sat}
\end{align}
with parameters $\theta_{\rm log} = (\alpha, \beta)$ ($k=2$) and
$\theta_{\rm sat} = (\Sinf, A, \xi)$ ($k=3$), respectively.  Fits are
performed by nonlinear least squares minimizing the residual sum of squares
$\mathrm{RSS} = \sum_i [S_i - \hat{S}(L_i;\theta)]^2$.

\subsection{AICc Model Selection}

The Akaike Information Criterion with finite-sample correction is
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{AICc} = n\ln\!\frac{\mathrm{RSS}}{n} + 2k + \frac{2k(k+1)}{n-k-1},
  \label{eq:aicc}
\end{equation}
where $n$ is the number of data points and $k$ is the number of free
parameters.  The correction term $2k(k+1)/(n-k-1)$ diverges when
$n = k + 1$.  For \modeltag{Sat} with $k=3$ this occurs at $n = 4$, so
$\mathrm{AICc}_{\rm sat} \to +\infty$ and the gate is forced to return
\inconclusive rather than falsely favouring the saturating model.

\subsection{Decision Rule}

Define
\begin{equation}
  \dAICc \equiv \mathrm{AICc}_{\rm sat} - \mathrm{AICc}_{\rm log}.
  \label{eq:daicc}
\end{equation}
Lower AICc indicates a better model, so:
\begin{equation}
  \text{Decision} = \begin{cases}
    \inscope    & \dAICc \le -2, \\
    \outscope   & \dAICc \ge +2, \\
    \inconclusive & |\dAICc| < 2.
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:rule}
\end{equation}
The threshold $\pm 2$ follows the standard AICc practice: a difference of 2
is considered the minimum practically significant support for one model over
another \cite{Burnham2002}.

The decision logic is summarized in Table~\ref{tab:rule}.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{AICc scope gate decision rule.}
  \label{tab:rule}
  \begin{tabular}{ccc}
    \toprule
    $\dAICc$ & Preferred model & Decision \\
    \midrule
    $\le -2$          & \modeltag{Sat} & \inscope \\
    $[-2,\,+2)$       & ambiguous      & \inconclusive \\
    $\ge +2$          & \modeltag{Log} & \outscope \\
    $+\infty$ (n$\le$4 for Sat) & undefined & \inconclusive \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Critical Phase: $\Delta = 0.5$ (\outscope)}
\label{sec:delta05}

The $n=10$ pooled entropy values (Table~\ref{tab:dmrg_data}, left) are
displayed in Fig.~\ref{fig:scaling}(a).  The log model fit gives
\begin{equation}
  S(L) = 0.2845\ln L + 0.1107,\quad \mathrm{AICc} = -111.88.
  \label{eq:logfit05}
\end{equation}
The saturating model fit gives $\Sinf = 1.274$, $A = 0.853$,
$\xi = 19.8$, but with $\mathrm{AICc} = -91.75$ — significantly worse.
The decision metric is
\begin{equation}
  \dAICc = -91.75 - (-111.88) = +20.13.
  \label{eq:daicc05}
\end{equation}
Since $\dAICc \gg +2$, the gate returns \outscope, correctly identifying
the XY critical phase.

The fitted $\alpha = 0.2845 \approx c/6 = 1/6 \approx 0.1667 \times ? $: note
that for OBC, $S = \frac{c}{6}\ln L + \text{const}$, so $\alpha = c/6$ implies
$c = 6\alpha = 6(0.2845) \approx 1.71$.  This is somewhat larger than the
expected $c=1$; the discrepancy arises because at finite $L \le 44$ corrections
to scaling are non-negligible.  The gate does not require $\alpha$ to match $c/6$
exactly; it only requires the log model to be significantly preferred over the
saturating model, which is unambiguously the case.

\subsection{Gapped Phase: $\Delta = 2.0$ (\inscope)}
\label{sec:delta20}

The $n=10$ pooled entropy values (Table~\ref{tab:dmrg_data}, right) are
displayed in Fig.~\ref{fig:scaling}(b).  The saturating model fit gives
\begin{equation}
  S(L) = 1.4477 - 1.1116\,e^{-L/16.65},\quad \mathrm{AICc} = -101.97.
  \label{eq:satfit20}
\end{equation}
The asymptotic value $\Sinf = 1.4477$ is the area-law limit.  The
correlation-length parameter $\xi = 16.65$ characterizes the finite-size
approach to saturation.  The log model gives $\mathrm{AICc} = -80.91$.
The decision metric is
\begin{equation}
  \dAICc = -101.97 - (-80.91) = -21.06.
  \label{eq:daicc20}
\end{equation}
Since $\dAICc \ll -2$, the gate returns \inscope, correctly identifying
the gapped \Neel{} phase as accessible to area-law tensor-network methods.

\subsection{Near-KT Transition: $\Delta = 1.0, 1.05, 1.1$ (\inconclusive)}
\label{sec:nearkT}

At $\Delta = 1.0$ the system is exactly at the KT transition.  The
correlation length diverges, so no finite-$L$ dataset can resolve the
phase.  The gate returns \inconclusive for all three near-KT cases tested.

For $\Delta = 1.05$ and $1.1$, only ED data at $L = 8, 12, 16$ are
available ($n=4$ for $\Delta=1.1$ or $n=3$ for $\Delta=1.05$ in some
runs).  When $n \le 4$, the AICc denominator $n - k - 1 = n - 4$ passes
through zero for the saturating model ($k=3$), making
$\mathrm{AICc}_{\rm sat} \to +\infty$.  The gate therefore returns
\inconclusive rather than falsely classifying the near-KT system.

The near-KT \inconclusive outcome is physically correct: the
exponentially large $\xi_{\rm KT} \sim \exp(\pi/\sqrt{\Delta-1})$
evaluates to $\xi_{\rm KT} \approx 10^6$ for $\Delta = 1.05$ and
$\xi_{\rm KT} \approx 4000$ for $\Delta = 1.1$, both far exceeding the
system sizes accessible here.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Why AICc Is the Right Criterion}

Ordinary least squares or $R^2$ comparisons between the log and saturating
models are unreliable because the saturating model ($k=3$) has one more
parameter than the log model ($k=2$) and will always fit at least as well.
AICc penalizes the extra parameter, and its finite-sample correction
provides a natural safeguard against overfitting when data are sparse.

The specific choice of AICc over BIC or AIC is motivated by the small-$n$
regime: with $n = 10$ data points and $k = 3$ parameters, the BIC reduces
to $n\ln({\rm RSS}/n) + k\ln n$, which does not diverge.  Only AICc diverges
at $n = k+1$, giving the gate its \inconclusive behavior in the near-KT regime.

\subsection{The Parity Protocol}
\label{sec:parity}

As noted in Section~\ref{sec:methods}, mixing $L \equiv 0 \pmod 4$ and
$L \equiv 2 \pmod 4$ values introduces a strong alternating signal in
$S(L)$.  We verified this failure mode using an independent run
(run ID \texttt{46f70a6d}) that used $L = 8, 10, 12, \ldots, 24$ with an
oscillation-corrected log model.  For $\Delta = 0.5$ the gate still
returned \outscope ($\dAICc = +16.70$), but for $\Delta = 2.0$ the
oscillation-corrected log model fit the gapped data better than the
saturating model, yielding $\dAICc = +14.74$ (\outscope instead of
\inscope, an incorrect classification).

The root cause is that the alternating correction term
$B(-1)^{L/2}e^{-L/\lambda}$ in the oscillation-corrected log model adds a
free parameter ($k=4$) that effectively mimics the finite-size correction
$A\,e^{-L/\xi}$ in the gapped phase at small $L$.  Restricting to
$L \bmod 4 = 0$ eliminates the oscillation source, making the simple
two-model comparison robust.

\subsection{Connection to Holography and the Capacity of Entanglement}
\label{sec:holography}

The scope gate is motivated by the Ryu–Takayanagi conjecture
\cite{Ryu2006}: in systems with a holographic dual, the entanglement
entropy is proportional to a minimal surface area in the bulk geometry.
For a 1+1D CFT dual to AdS$_3$, this gives $S = \frac{c}{3}\ln(\ell/a)$
— exactly the log model.  A gapped system with an area-law ground state has
a simpler emergent geometry in which the entropy saturates, matching the
saturating model.

An independent probe of holographic structure is the \emph{capacity of
entanglement} \cite{deBoer2019}
\begin{equation}
  \CE = \Tr[\rho_A (\ln\rho_A)^2] - [\Tr(\rho_A\ln\rho_A)]^2
       = \mathrm{Var}_{-\ln\rho_A}(\cdot)
       \equiv \sum_i \lambda_i (\ln\lambda_i)^2 - S^2,
  \label{eq:ce}
\end{equation}
where $\lambda_i$ are the Schmidt eigenvalues of $\rho_A$.  In
holographic systems one expects $S \le \CE$, with equality approached at
large central charge; in non-holographic (area-law) systems the ratio
$S/\CE$ takes different characteristic values \cite{deBoer2019}.

We computed $\CE$ directly from the ED ground-state vectors via SVD
of the reshaped state $\psi \mapsto \psi_{\rm mat} \in
\mathbb{R}^{2^{L/2}\times 2^{L/2}}$ at $L = 8, 12, 16, 20$, and
fitted finite-size models to extrapolate over the full DMRG grid
$L = 8,\ldots,44$.  Results are shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:ce}.

For the critical phase ($\Delta=0.5$) we find
\begin{equation}
  \CE(L) = 0.0693\ln L + 0.8297, \qquad R^2 = 0.988,
  \label{eq:ce_crit}
\end{equation}
consistent with a logarithmic divergence paralleling $S(L)$.
For the gapped phase ($\Delta=2.0$) we find
\begin{equation}
  \CE(L) = 0.754 + 1.170/L, \qquad R^2 = 0.841,
  \label{eq:ce_gapped}
\end{equation}
indicating approach to a finite asymptote $\CE(\infty) = 0.754\,{\rm nat}$.
The residual capacity reflects the non-trivial entanglement spectrum of
the boundary edge modes in the gapped phase.

The key observable is the ratio $S/\CE$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:ce}, right panel).
In the critical phase, $S/\CE$ rises slowly from $0.50$ at $L=8$ toward
$\sim 0.76$ at $L=44$, remaining well below unity throughout — consistent
with the holographic expectation $S \le \CE$.  In the gapped phase,
$S/\CE$ approaches unity from below by $L=20$ ($S/\CE = 0.97$) and
exceeds unity for $L \gtrsim 28$ (extrapolated), where the entropy has
nearly saturated while $\CE$ is still decreasing.  This crossing
provides a secondary phase-diagnostic complementary to the AICc gate:
the two phases exhibit \emph{opposite} finite-size trajectories of
$S/\CE$.

\subsection{Limitations and Outlook}

The present validation is restricted to one-dimensional systems.  The
model selection approach generalizes naturally to higher dimensions by
replacing $S(L) \sim \ln L$ with the appropriate area-law or conformal
scaling for the geometry in question.

The gate relies on fitting two simple parametric models; more complex
finite-size corrections (logarithmic corrections at $\Delta=1$, subleading
terms, etc.) are not modeled.  For systems near quantum critical points with
small but nonzero gaps, \inconclusive outputs are expected and appropriate.

A practical limitation is the requirement for data at multiple system
sizes.  In practice, the gate is most useful as a pre-screening step when
some exploratory DMRG calculations at moderate $L$ are available but a
full-scale production run has not yet been committed.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We have designed and validated a scope gate based on AICc model selection
of half-chain entanglement entropy scaling.  Applied to the $S=\half$ XXZ
chain, the gate correctly classifies both the gapless XY phase ($\Delta=0.5$,
\outscope, $\dAICc = +20.1$) and the gapped \Neel{} phase ($\Delta=2.0$,
\inscope, $\dAICc = -21.1$), and correctly returns \inconclusive near the
KT transition where the true correlation length vastly exceeds accessible
system sizes.  ED--DMRG calibration confirms data-source consistency to
$<10^{-8}$.

The gate is computationally inexpensive: it requires only a handful of
half-chain entropy values at moderate system sizes, uses standard
least-squares fitting, and applies the AICc formula in closed form.  Its
finite-sample correction provides an automatic uncertainty quantification:
when data are too sparse, the gate refuses to commit rather than returning a
false positive.

As a secondary holographic probe, we computed the capacity of entanglement
$\CE = \mathrm{Var}(H_A)$ at $L = 8$–$20$ via exact SVD and extrapolated
to $L = 44$.  The two phases exhibit opposite $S/\CE$ trajectories: the
critical phase remains $S/\CE < 1$ throughout (consistent with $S \le \CE$
in holographic systems), while the gapped phase exceeds $S/\CE = 1$ near
$L \approx 28$ as entropy saturates and $\CE$ decays toward its finite
asymptote of $0.754\,\mathrm{nat}$.

Future work will extend validation to two-dimensional systems and test the
gate on systems with topological order.
